Books Finished

Demons

Fyodor Dostoevsky

The character of Kirillov is particularly intriguing. Despite valuing his life and having a longstanding friendship with Shatov, he chooses to commit suicide for completely philosophical reasons. This somehow makes Stavrogin's suicide more like an ironic escape.

Nineteen Eighty-Four

George Orwell

Personally I think it is the most depressive one amoung the Dystopian Trilogy, perhaps the society described here is closer to our real life and the ending is also more real. I'm a bit confused with Julia's characterization, actually I feel like the heroines of the three books are all hard to unerstand.

The Brothers Karamazov

Fyodor Dostoevsky

I always find it difficult to empathize with Dostoevsky's characters, perhaps because I'm an atheist. And this, as the first Dostoevsky's book I've read, is especially so. I really need to read it again some day to understand Ivan's inner struggle. By the way, I really like its Russian musical, the stage design is concise and powerful.

The Idiot

Fyodor Dostoevsky

Somehow I find Nastasya a bit similar to Grushenka, while Myshkin can't love her back as Dimitri due to his personality. This is the thir book of Dostoevsky I read and I start to realize that his writing tyle is really "insane", but this insanity can be extremely impressive. In the end, just like Dream of the Red Chamber, nothing left, only a landscape desolate and bare.

The Master and Margarita

Mikhail Bulgakov

It reminds me of the firt time I read The Lord of the Rings. It doesn't have many big problems to discuss and I only need to play around Moscow together with the devils. The underlying plot is disturbing, but the overall story calms me that there are many other important things not related to the so called evil and good.

One Hundred Years of Solitude

Gabriel García Márquez

It is more like a dream than a story. Each member of the Buendía family is vivid with unique traits and quirks. I can hardly tells any detailed interaction between them but reading the book feels like witnessing a magical world fade away. Unfortunately, I don't have much interest to dig into Latin American history to understand all metaphors within at current.
